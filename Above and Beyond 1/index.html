INTERNET OF THINGS

- Dipansh Garg

Know the author

IoT or Internet of Things is a network of objects or devices reffered to as things that are integrated with sensors, software and other technologies, with the purpose of automating dily life tasks and improving efficiency. It uses low-cost computing, the cloud, big data, analytics, and mobile technologies, physical things can share and collect data with minimal human intervention.

What can be built using IoT?

IoT has applications in almost all of the major/minor fields. Here are a few examples:

  1. Smart Homes: IoT-enabled devices like smart thermostats, smart lighting, and smart security systems can be controlled remotely through a smartphone app or a voice assistant, making homes more energy-efficient, secure, and convenient.
  2. Healthcare: IoT devices like wearable sensors, remote patient monitoring systems, and smart pills can track patient data and provide real-time insights, helping doctors diagnose and treat diseases more effectively.
  3. Industrial IoT: IoT devices can monitor and optimize industrial processes, improve worker safety, and reduce equipment downtime. Examples include predictive maintenance, automated inventory management, and connected logistics.
  4. Smart Agriculture: IoT sensors can monitor soil moisture, temperature, and nutrient levels, helping farmers optimize crop yields and reduce water usage.
  5. Smart Cities: IoT devices can improve traffic management, reduce energy consumption, and enhance public safety. Examples include smart traffic lights, smart waste management, and smart parking systems.
  6. Retail: IoT devices can enhance the shopping experience by providing personalized recommendations, optimizing inventory management, and enabling seamless checkout processes.
  7. Transportation: IoT devices can improve the safety and efficiency of transportation systems. Examples include connected vehicles, smart traffic management systems, and real-time transit information.


Skillsets required:

  1. Programming: IoT systems require programming expertise in languages such as C++, Python, Java, and JavaScript.
  2. Embedded Systems: Understanding of embedded systems, microcontrollers, and sensors is critical to developing IoT devices.
  3. Networking: Knowledge of networking protocols such as TCP/IP, MQTT, and Zigbee is essential for IoT developers.
  4. Cloud computing: Cloud computing is a vital part of IoT, and understanding cloud platforms like AWS, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud is a must-have skill.
  5. Security: With the growth of connected devices, security threats are a significant concern. IoT developers must understand security threats and protocols like encryption, authentication, and secure coding practices.
  6. Data Analytics: IoT devices generate a vast amount of data, and data analytics skills are essential for managing and analyzing this data.
  7. Project Management: IoT projects can be complex, and project management skills are essential for overseeing projects and ensuring they are completed on time and within budget.
